If you want a structured program with real momentum: Rootd

4.8★ across 10,000 ratings, with a free tier that already covers the panic button and core breathing tools.

The Rootr panic button is built for the exact moment panic spikes, and the journal helps you spot patterns afterward. Its free tier is genuinely generous — pay only if you want the full lesson set and lifetime access.

If you want a voice talking you through it, plus other people: Dare

4.8★ across 6,600 ratings, built specifically around audio SOS guides and live group coaching.

Dare's audio library is the draw — a real voice walking you through panic in the moment, not just text on a screen. One honest note from reviews: reminder notifications are a recurring review theme, so expect some nudging to open the app.

If your budget is zero: PanicShield

4.9★ from 50 ratings — a small review base, but a genuinely free app with no ads and no in-app purchases at all.

PanicShield pairs paced breathing with real exposure-training exercises built on CBT principles. It's a small team and a small review count, but what's there is honestly worth trying before you pay anyone anything.

The honest bottom line

Panic tools shouldn't rent-seek from the exact moment you're most vulnerable. Free and one-time options now genuinely compete with subscriptions on quality, not just price. Whatever you pick, the real test isn't the app store page — it's whether you'll actually open it mid-panic. Pick that one.
